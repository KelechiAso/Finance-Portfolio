My Take on Financial Planning

I've been learning that the financial planning process is a comprehensive, step-by-step approach to managing a person's or family's finances to achieve their life goals. It's not a one-time thing but a continuous journey, and I see it as an essential tool for personal and family empowerment, especially in a place like Magboro, Ogun State, Nigeria, where the economic environment can be uncertain.

Understanding the Financial Planning Process

For me, financial planning is a structured way to make sure my money is working for me. It's about setting clear objectives, creating a roadmap to get there, and regularly checking in on my progress. The process is broken down into several key steps:

Step 1: Establishing the Relationship and Goals

This initial step is about setting a clear foundation. If I were working with a client, we'd have a clear discussion to set expectations and responsibilities. For me personally, it's about taking a moment to reflect on my own goals. This is where I define my objectives, making sure they're **SMART**—Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. For example, instead of just "save for a house," I'd set a goal to "save ₦5,000,000 for a house down payment in four years."

Step 2: Gathering Data and Analyzing Status

This is the most critical and comprehensive step. I collect all the financial information, like income, expenses, assets, and liabilities. I then analyze this data to see where I stand relative to my goals. This involves:

  • Cash Flow Analysis: I figure out where my money is coming from and where it's going.
  • Net Worth Statement: I calculate my assets minus my liabilities to get a snapshot of my current financial health.
  • Risk Analysis: I evaluate my own risk tolerance for investments and assess my insurance needs.

Step 3: Developing and Presenting the Plan

Based on my analysis, I would create a comprehensive roadmap. The plan includes specific recommendations, such as adjusting my savings rate or rebalancing my investment portfolio. I also make sure to present different scenarios with clear explanations of the assumptions made, like projected investment returns or inflation rates.

Step 4: Implementing and Monitoring the Plan

Once the plan is in place, it's a continuous process. I'll guide the implementation and then schedule regular check-ins to monitor my progress. The plan isn't static; I'll make necessary changes based on how my investments are performing or if any major life events occur, such as a new job or a family change.
